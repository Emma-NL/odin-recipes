Spaghetti Carbonara

Discription

Spaghetti carbonara is a traditional Italian pasta dish. It's rich, creamy and can be served at any occasion! This recipe only takes 40 minutes and is amazing for serving a lot of people all at once.

Ingredients

Original recipe serves 8 people.

  • 1 pound spaghetti
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 8 slices of bacon
  • 1 onion
  • 1 clove garlic
  • 4 large eggs
  • ½ cup grated parmesan cheese
  • ¼ cup dry white whine
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • fresh parsley for garnish

Steps

  1. Bring a pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the spaghetti in boiling water and stir occasionally. Cook the spaghetti for 12 minutes. Once done, drain the water and toss the spaghetti in 1 tablespoon of olive oil. Set spaghetti aside.
  2. Dice the bacon. Place the diced bacon in a skillit over medium heat. Cook and stir it for about 10 minutes and make sure the bacon is evenly browned. Dry bacon on paper towels and reserve 2 tablespoons of bacon fat in the skillit.
  3. Add 1 tablespoon of olive oil to the bacon fat in the skillit. Chop the onion and mince the garlic. Place the onion and garlic in the skillit and let it cook until union is translucent. Add white whine and let cook for 1 minute.
  4. Return the cooked bacon in the skillit and add the cooked spaghetti. Toss to coat. If it seems dry, add some more olive oil. Beat your eggs and add it into the skillit. Cook the eggs by tossing constantly with a tong and make sure the eggs don't overcook! Add 1/2 cup of parmesan cheese and toss. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Serve the spaghetti with some fresh parsley and extra parmesan cheese. Enjoy!
