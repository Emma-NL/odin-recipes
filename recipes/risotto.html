Mushroom Risotto

Discription

Risotto is a traditional Italian dish. It's cooked in broth to make it creamy and flavorful. The umami mushrooms and the parmesan cheese make it irresistible. This recipe is amazing as is, but is definitely a good base to add protein!

Ingredients

Original recipe serves 6 people.

  • 6 cups chicken broth
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 pound portobello mushrooms
  • 1 pound white mushrooms
  • 2 medium shallots
  • 1½ cups Arborio rice
  • ½ cup dry white wine
  • ⅓ cup grated parmesan cheese
  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • 3 tablespoons chopped chives
  • salt and pepper to tase

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients
  2. Warm the broth in a saucepan over low heat.
  3. Warm 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a deep large sauce pan over medium-high heat. Slice the portobello and white mushrooms and add them to the pan. Cook them for about 3 minutes. When finished, set the mushrooms aside.
  4. Heat up the remaining tablespoon of olive oil. Chop the shallots and add them in to the pan. Let this cook for about 1 minute. After that you add the rice and cook for about 2 minutes.
  5. Add the wine and stir continuously until it is completely absorbed. Next, pour in ½ cup of warm broth and keep stirring until the liquid is absorbed.
  6. Keep adding broth, ½ cup at a time, while stirring. Wait until the liquid is absorbed each time. Continue until the rice is tender but still slightly firm, about 15-20 minutes.
  7. Remove the pan from the heat. Add the mushrooms, parmesan cheese, butter, and chives. Mix everything together and season with salt and pepper.
  8. Serve the risotto and enjoy!
